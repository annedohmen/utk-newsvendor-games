In this experiment, you have the role of an inventory manager. You must decide how many units of a product you want to order and stock to sell them to your customers.

You are making these decisions over multiple rounds. In every round, you are deciding on an order quantity.

You are making your decision under uncertainty, i.e., at the time of the order decision you do not know the exact demand of the period. However, you do know the probability distribution of the demand. The demand for your product will be show by a distribution curve, shown on the decision-making page. Demand is independent between rounds.

You have no starting inventory in your warehouse at the beginning of the game. You will order units which then get delivered before demand is realized. Any leftover inventory after demand is realized will be carried over to the next round (starting inventory). Starting inventory and order quantity sum up to the available inventory as follows:

{#      #} available inventory = starting inventory + order quantity

Note that you cannot order more than 1,000 units per round.

Holding costs occur for holding inventory which is carried over between rounds. E.g., if your available inventory is 90 units but demand is only 80 units, you can only 80 sell units (because demand was only 80). The left-over 10 units are stocked in inventory and holding costs are charged for these. Holding costs are charged for the resulting left-over 10 units that are stocked in inventory. If you purchased too few units in a period to fulfill this period’s demand, the unfulfilled demand is lost. You cannot reorder within a period or shift demand to later periods. E.g., if your available inventory is only 30 units and demand is 80 units, you can only sell 30 units (since you did not order more).

You are trying to maximize your Cash Flow (CF) in this experiment. The CF is calculated as follows:

CF = Selling price x units sold - purchasing price x order quantity – holding costs * holding inventory

Note that the units sold is the minimum of demand and available inventory. You receive the selling price for every unit sold. From this amount, you need to deduct the purchasing costs for the products bought. Additionally, you need to deduct the holding costs for the holding inventory.

In this experiment, your costs will be displayed on the decision page.
